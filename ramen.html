Ramen

Ramen is a noodle dish made with wheat noodles served in a flavorful broth, usually based on soy sauce, miso, or pork bone (tonkotsu), and topped with ingredients like sliced pork, boiled eggs, seaweed, and green onions. It is known for its rich taste and variety of regional styles, making it a very popular comfort food. Ramen originated in Japan but was heavily influenced by Chinese noodle dishes that were introduced in the late 19th and early 20th centuries. It became especially popular after World War II and later developed into a cultural icon of Japanese cuisine, with many different local variations found across Japan and now worldwide.
