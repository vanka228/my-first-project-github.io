Pasta

Pasta is a traditional dish made from unleavened dough of wheat flour mixed with water or eggs, formed into various shapes and usually cooked by boiling. It is commonly served with sauces such as tomato, cream, or pesto, and can also include meat, vegetables, or cheese. Pasta is known for its versatility and is a staple food in many households around the world. Pasta originated in Italy, where it became a central part of national cuisine, although similar noodle-like foods existed in ancient civilizations like China and the Middle East. It developed into many regional varieties in Italy and spread globally over time, especially through Italian migration and international cuisine, becoming one of the most popular and widely eaten dishes worldwide.
