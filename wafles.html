Waffles

Waffles are a sweet or savory dish made from batter cooked between two patterned plates, creating a crispy outside with a soft inside and a characteristic grid shape. They are often served with toppings like syrup, fruit, whipped cream, chocolate, or even fried chicken in some modern variations. Waffles are popular as a breakfast or dessert food due to their texture and versatility. Waffles originated in medieval Europe, particularly in regions like Belgium and France, where early versions were cooked over open fires using metal plates. Belgian waffles later became especially famous for their light, airy texture and deep pockets for toppings. Today, waffles are enjoyed worldwide in many styles, from classic European versions to American street-food and café variations.
